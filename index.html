LO!

I am a senior pursuing B.S. in Computer Science and Cognitive Science at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. My interest includes Programming Languages (PL), Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), and Machine Learning.

During the 2022-23 cycle, I am applying to Masters programs in Computer Science and Engineering. I am expected to start graduate program in Fall 2023 and graduate between April 2024 and April 2025.

Since May 2021, I have conducted research supervised by Professor Xinyu Wang in the Michigan Intelligent Programming Lab (MIPL).

I am currently a part-time intern at Yerbba, and my role is Software Engineer.

I am the instructional aide for EECS 481 (Software Engineering) at the University of Michigan during this semester (Fall 22).

Highlighted Projects

Here are some projects I did in various courses or for fun.

Search Engine

In this Major Design Experience (MDE) / capstone course taught by Nicole Hamilton, we work in a team of six to build an entire Internet search engine from scratch in C++.

RateMProfessors

This tool simplifies the process of looking up professors' Rate My Professors page on UM's LSA Course Guide.

Image Colorization

This is the final project for EECS 442, Computer Vision. In a group of four, we implemented a neural network that colorizes greyscale images in Python.

DJ 183

As the final group project for EECS 183, Elementary Programming Concepts, this is a web-based "Guess-the-Song" game written in Python(flask), HTML, and CSS. The permission to use music from billboard, unfortunately, is ended at the end of that semester. To make this game up and running again may require paying for ASCAP or/and BMI license. If you wish to play it again, please consider this.
